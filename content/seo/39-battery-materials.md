## Cathode Material Processing

### NMC (Nickel-Manganese-Cobalt)

**Typical Composition:** LiNiₓMnᵧCo₂O₂

| Parameter | Specification | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| D50 | 5-10 μm | Optimal for electrode |
| D10 | >2 μm | Prevent separator puncture |
| D90 | <20 μm | Consistent coating |
| Tap density | >2.2 g/cm³ | Energy density |
| BET | 0.2-0.8 m²/g | Surface area |

**Grinding Parameters:**

| Parameter | Setting | Rationale |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Media | Y-TZP zirconia | No metal contamination |
| Jar | Zirconia | Matching |
| RPM | 400-500 | Controlled energy |
| BPR | 10:1 | Standard |
| Liquid | NMP or water | Dispersion |
| Time | 1-4 hours | To specification |

### LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate)

**Characteristics:**
- Olivine structure
- Lower voltage (3.2V vs 3.7V)
- Higher safety
- Lower cost

**Particle Size Requirements:**

| Grade | D50 | Application |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| Standard | 1-3 μm | Consumer electronics |
| High power | 0.5-1 μm | Power tools, EVs |
| Energy | 3-5 μm | Energy storage |

**Processing Considerations:**
- Carbon coating often required
- Particle size affects rate capability
- Tap density critical for energy density

### LCO (Lithium Cobalt Oxide)

**Applications:** Consumer electronics

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| D50 | 5-15 μm |
| D10 | >3 μm |
| Tap density | >2.5 g/cm³ |

## Anode Material Processing

### Graphite Processing

**Natural vs Synthetic Graphite:**

| Property | Natural | Synthetic |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Cost | Lower | Higher |
| Capacity | 340-370 mAh/g | 350-365 mAh/g |
| Cycle life | Good | Excellent |
| Grinding | Easier | Harder |

**Spheroidization Process:**

```
Stage 1: Grinding
Flake graphite → Irregular particles (D50 ~10μm)

Stage 2: Shaping
Impact milling → Rounded particles

Stage 3: Classification
Remove fines and oversize

Result: Spherical graphite, D50 10-20μm
```

**Grinding Parameters for Graphite:**

| Parameter | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Media | Zirconia or alumina |
| RPM | 400-500 |
| Atmosphere | Inert preferred |
| PCA | None needed |
| Target | D50 = 10-20 μm |

### Silicon Anode Processing

**Challenges:**
- Large volume expansion (300%)
- Particle size critical
- Surface oxide layer
- Cost sensitivity

**Nanostructuring Approaches:**

| Approach | Size | Method |
|----------|------|--------|
| Nanoparticles | 50-200 nm | High-energy milling |
| Porous silicon | 1-5 μm | Etching |
| Si/C composite | Variable | Coating |
| Nanowires | Diameter 50-100 nm | Growth |

**Ball Milling for Silicon Nanoparticles:**

| Parameter | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Media | WC or ZrO₂ |
| BPR | 20:1 |
| RPM | 500-600 |
| Atmosphere | Argon |
| Time | 8-20 hours |
| Result | 50-200 nm |

### Hard Carbon

**Applications:** Sodium-ion batteries

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| D50 | 5-10 μm |
| Surface area | 2-10 m²/g |
| Pore size | 2-50 nm |

## Solid Electrolyte Processing

### Sulfide Solid Electrolytes

**Common Materials:**
- Li₂S-P₂S₅ (LPS)
- Li₆PS₅Cl (Argyrodite)
- Li₃PS₄

**Processing Challenges:**
- Air sensitivity (H₂S generation)
- Must process in inert atmosphere
- Mechanical activation required
- Precise stoichiometry critical

**Ball Milling Conditions:**

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Atmosphere | Argon glove box |
| O₂ level | <1 ppm |
| H₂O level | <1 ppm |
| Media | Zirconia |
| BPR | 20:1 |
| Time | 10-40 hours |
| Product | Glass-ceramic |

### Oxide Solid Electrolytes

**Garnet-Type (LLZO):**
- Li₇La₃Zr₂O₁₂
- High ionic conductivity
- Air stable
- Requires high temperature sintering

**NASICON-Type (LATP):**
- Li₁.₃Al₀.₃Ti₁.₇(PO₄)₃
- Good chemical stability
- Moderate conductivity

## Contamination Control

### Critical Contaminants

| Contaminant | Source | Impact | Limit |
|-------------|--------|--------|-------|
| Iron | Steel media | Capacity fade | <50 ppm |
| Copper | Equipment | Dendrite growth | <10 ppm |
| Nickel | Equipment | Safety risk | <10 ppm |
| Chromium | Stainless | Impedance rise | <50 ppm |
| Zirconia | Media | Generally acceptable | <1000 ppm |

### Contamination Prevention

**Equipment Selection:**

| Component | Recommended | Avoid |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Grinding media | Y-TZP | Steel |
| Jars | Zirconia, alumina | Steel |
| Tools | Ceramic/plastic | Metal |
| Environment | Clean room | General lab |

**Cleaning Protocol:**
1. Dedicated equipment for battery materials
2. Acid wash (if appropriate)
3. Deionized water rinse
4. Drying in clean environment
5. Contamination testing before use

## FAQ

**Q: Why is metal contamination so critical for batteries?**
A: Metal ions cause side reactions, capacity fade, and safety issues like dendrite formation.

**Q: Can I use steel grinding media for battery materials?**
A: Generally no. Use zirconia or other ceramic media to prevent iron contamination.

**Q: What's the ideal particle size for cathode materials?**
A: D50 of 5-10 μm is typical, balancing energy density, power, and processing.

**Q: How do I process air-sensitive solid electrolytes?**
A: Use glove box with <1 ppm O₂/H₂O, sealed mills, and proper handling protocols.

**Q: Why is silicon ground to nanoparticles?**
A: To accommodate volume expansion during cycling and improve cycle life.
