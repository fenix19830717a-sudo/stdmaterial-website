## Wear Mechanisms

### Types of Wear

**1. Abrasive Wear**
- Mechanism: Hard particles scratch surface
- Rate: Proportional to particle hardness
- Mitigation: Harder liner material

**2. Impact Wear**
- Mechanism: Ball impacts cause fatigue
- Rate: Increases with ball size and speed
- Mitigation: Tough materials, rubber

**3. Corrosive Wear**
- Mechanism: Chemical attack + mechanical wear
- Rate: Depends on pH and abrasiveness
- Mitigation: Corrosion-resistant materials

### Wear Rate Factors

| Factor | Impact on Wear Rate |
|--------|---------------------|
| Ore hardness | +20% per Mohs unit |
| Ball size | +30% per 25mm increase |
| Mill speed | +15% per 10% RPM increase |
| Feed size | +10% per 10mm increase |
| Slurry density | -5% per 10% increase |

## Installation & Maintenance

### Installation Best Practices

**Pre-Installation:**
1. Clean shell thoroughly
2. Inspect for damage
3. Measure and record shell thickness
4. Plan liner layout

**Installation Sequence:**
1. Install center liners first
2. Work toward ends
3. Maintain proper gaps (3-5mm)
4. Torque bolts to specification
5. Check bolt tightness after 24 hours

## FAQ

**Q: How often should liners be replaced?**
A: When thickness reduces to 50% of original, or when grinding efficiency drops significantly. Typically 18-36 months depending on application.

**Q: Can different liner materials be combined?**
A: Yes. Common practice: steel in high-impact zones, rubber in grinding zones, ceramic for fine grinding sections.

**Q: What causes premature liner failure?**
A: Common causes: incorrect installation, overtightened bolts, feed size too large, mill speed too high, or corrosive environment.

**Q: Are ceramic liners suitable for large mills?**
A: Generally limited to smaller mills (<3m) due to impact concerns. Metal-ceramic composites are used for larger mills.

**Q: How do I reduce liner wear?**
A: Optimize ball size distribution, control feed size, maintain proper mill speed, use appropriate liner material, and ensure even wear pattern.
