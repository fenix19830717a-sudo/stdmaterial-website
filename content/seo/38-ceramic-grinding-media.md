## Alumina Grinding Media

### Alumina Grades

**By Purity Level:**

| Grade | Al₂O₃ Content | Hardness (HV) | Density (g/cm³) | Cost |
|-------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|------|
| 85% | 85% | 1200 | 3.4 | Low |
| 92% | 92% | 1500 | 3.6 | Medium |
| 95% | 95% | 1600 | 3.7 | Medium-High |
| 99% | 99% | 1800 | 3.9 | High |

### Properties and Applications

**85% Alumina:**
- Good wear resistance
- Economical
- Applications: General mineral processing

**92-95% Alumina:**
- Excellent wear resistance
- Good balance of properties
- Applications: Ceramic bodies, pigments

**99%+ Alumina:**
- Superior wear resistance
- Chemical inertness
- Applications: Electronics, pharmaceuticals

## Zirconia Grinding Media

### Types of Zirconia

**Y-TZP (Yttria-Stabilized Tetragonal Zirconia Polycrystal):**

| Property | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| ZrO₂ content | 94-95% | Balance is Y₂O₃ |
| Density | 6.0 g/cm³ | High for ceramics |
| Hardness | 1300 HV | Very wear resistant |
| Fracture toughness | 8-10 MPa·m½ | High for ceramic |
| Best for | Premium applications | Nanoparticles |

**Ce-TZP (Ceria-Stabilized):**

| Property | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| ZrO₂ content | 88% | Balance is CeO₂ |
| Density | 5.8 g/cm³ | Slightly lower |
| Hardness | 1100 HV | Good wear resistance |
| Cost | 30% less than Y-TZP | Cost-effective |
| Best for | General applications | Cost-sensitive |

**ZTA (Zirconia Toughened Alumina):**

| Property | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Composition | Al₂O₃ + 10-20% ZrO₂ | Composite |
| Density | 4.2 g/cm³ | Intermediate |
| Hardness | 1600 HV | High |
| Cost | Medium | Good value |
| Best for | High temperature | Thermal shock |

### Zirconia Advantages

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| High density | Efficient energy transfer |
| High hardness | Low wear, long life |
| Chemical inertness | No contamination |
| Smooth surface | Low adhesion |
| High toughness | Resists chipping |

## Silica and Other Ceramics

### Silica (SiO₂) Media

**Properties:**

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Density | 2.5 g/cm³ |
| Hardness | 550 HV |
| Purity | 99%+ SiO₂ |
| Cost | Very low |

**Applications:**
- Paint and coating industry
- Low-value products
- Where contamination acceptable
- Historical applications

**Limitations:**
- High wear rate
- Not for hard materials
- Lower grinding efficiency

### Specialty Ceramic Media

**Steatite (Talc-based):**
- Density: 2.7 g/cm³
- Hardness: 400 HV
- Low cost
- Limited applications

**Porcelain:**
- Density: 2.4 g/cm³
- Hardness: 500 HV
- Decorative/media
- Very low wear resistance

**Glass Beads:**
- Density: 2.5 g/cm³
- Hardness: 550 HV
- Very economical
- Limited to soft materials

**Silicon Carbide (SiC):**
- Density: 3.2 g/cm³
- Hardness: 2500 HV
- Very hard material
- For ultra-hard grinding

### Application-Specific Selection

**Pharmaceutical Industry:**
- Requirement: Zero contamination, FDA approved
- Recommendation: Y-TZP (GMP grade)
- Alternative: Alumina 99.5% (cost sensitive)

**Battery Industry:**
- Requirement: No metal contamination
- Recommendation: Y-TZP or Ce-TZP
- For NMC: Essential to use zirconia

**Ceramic Industry:**
- Requirement: Cost-effective, consistent
- Recommendation: Alumina 92%
- Large volume applications

**Mining Industry:**
- Requirement: Lowest cost, acceptable wear
- Recommendation: Alumina 85%
- High volume, low value products

**Research Laboratories:**
- Requirement: Versatility, purity
- Recommendation: Y-TZP set
- Various jar materials to match

## FAQ

**Q: What's the difference between alumina and zirconia media?**
A: Zirconia is denser (6.0 vs 3.9 g/cm³), tougher, and wears longer, but costs more. Alumina is harder and better for high temperature.

**Q: Can I use alumina media for grinding alumina powder?**
A: Technically yes, but you'll have contamination. For high purity, use different material (zirconia) or same-grade alumina.

**Q: How long does ceramic media last?**
A: Depends on material and application: Alumina 85% ~500 hours, Y-TZP ~5000 hours under normal conditions.

**Q: Is silica media still used?**
A: Rarely in modern applications due to poor wear resistance. Mostly legacy or very cost-sensitive applications.

**Q: Can ceramic media be used for wet grinding?**
A: Yes, all ceramic media are suitable for wet grinding. They're chemically inert to most liquids.
