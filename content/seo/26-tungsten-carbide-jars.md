## Why Tungsten Carbide?

Tungsten carbide (WC) represents the pinnacle of grinding jar materials, offering unmatched hardness and wear resistance for the most demanding applications.

### Key Advantages

| Property | WC Value | Benefit |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Hardness | 1600-1800 HV | Grinds materials up to 9 Mohs |
| Compressive strength | 5500 MPa | Withstands high impact forces |
| Density | 14.5-15.0 g/cm³ | Efficient energy transfer |
| Young's modulus | 650 GPa | Minimal deformation |
| Thermal conductivity | 84 W/m·K | Effective heat dissipation |

### When to Choose Tungsten Carbide

**Ideal Applications:**
- Hard metals and alloys (Ti, W, Mo)
- Non-oxide ceramics (SiC, B₄C, Si₃N₄)
- Minerals and ores (quartz, corundum)
- Mechanical alloying
- High-energy milling

## Material Properties & Composition

### Chemical Composition

**Standard WC-Co Composition:**

| Component | Percentage | Function |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Tungsten carbide (WC) | 94% | Hard phase, wear resistance |
| Cobalt (Co) | 6% | Binder phase, toughness |

### Grade Variations

| YG8 | 8% | 1500 HV | Impact applications |

## Applications & Best Uses

### Hard Metal Processing

**Titanium and Alloys:**
- Grades: Ti-6Al-4V, CP Ti
- Typical result: <10 μm in 4 hours
- Media: WC balls matching jar

**Tungsten and Heavy Alloys:**
- Density matching principle
- High energy requirement
- Excellent contamination control

### Ceramic Processing

| Ceramic | Hardness | WC Suitability | Notes |
|---------|----------|----------------|-------|
| Silicon carbide (SiC) | 9.5 Mohs | Excellent | Best choice |
| Boron carbide (B₄C) | 9.3 Mohs | Excellent | Long grinding time |
| Silicon nitride (Si₃N₄) | 8.5 Mohs | Excellent | Preferred media |
| Alumina (Al₂O₃) | 9.0 Mohs | Good | Zirconia alternative |

### Mechanical Alloying

**Process Requirements:**
- Extended grinding times (20-100 hours)
- High impact energy
- Contamination-free environment

**WC Advantages:**
- Minimal wear during long runs
- No iron contamination
- Stable performance

### Mineral Processing

**Hard Rock Applications:**
- Quartz grinding
- Corundum processing
- Diamond polishing compounds
- Abrasive powder production

## Maintenance & Care

### Cleaning Protocol

**After Each Use:**
1. Empty grinding media and powder
2. Rinse with appropriate solvent
3. Wipe with lint-free cloth
4. Inspect for damage
5. Store dry with protective coating

**Recommended Solvents:**
| Contamination Type | Solvent | Notes |
|-------------------|---------|-------|
| Organic residues | Acetone or ethanol | Quick evaporation |
| Metallic powders | Dilute acid (5%) | Rinse thoroughly |
| Ceramic powders | Water with surfactant | Avoid harsh abrasives |

### Inspection Schedule

| Frequency | Inspection Point | Acceptance Criteria |
|-----------|------------------|---------------------|
| Daily | Visual surface check | No visible cracks |
| Weekly | Wall thickness | >90% original |
| Monthly | Seal surface | Smooth, no scoring |
| Quarterly | Internal volume | Within 2% specification |

### Storage Recommendations

**Proper Storage:**
- Clean and dry thoroughly
- Apply light oil film (prevent oxidation)
- Store in protective case
- Avoid stacking heavy objects
- Temperature: 15-25°C, humidity <60%

## Safety Considerations

### Handling Precautions

**Weight Hazard:**
- 1L WC jar weighs ~4kg empty
- Use proper lifting technique
- Two-person lift for large jars

**Sharp Edges:**
- Wear cut-resistant gloves
- Inspect for chips before handling
- Dispose of damaged jars properly

### Health Considerations

**Cobalt Exposure:**
- Minimal risk with proper handling
- Wear dust mask when cleaning
- Wash hands after handling
- Cobalt is binder, not free metal

### Emergency Procedures

**If Jar Cracks:**
1. Stop machine immediately
2. Do not open until fully stopped
3. Inspect for media escape
4. Document incident
5. Replace jar before restart

## FAQ

**Q: Can WC jars be used for wet grinding?**
A: Yes, WC is chemically resistant to most solvents. Avoid strong oxidizers and hydrofluoric acid.

**Q: What's the maximum temperature for WC jars?**
A: Standard cobalt-bonded WC: 400°C continuous. Nickel-bonded grades: 600°C.

**Q: How do I prevent cobalt leaching?**
A: Use nickel-bonded grades (YN series) for acidic environments. Standard WC-Co is stable in neutral pH.

**Q: Can damaged WC jars be repaired?**
A: Minor damage to sealing surfaces can be ground. Cracked jars should be replaced due to safety concerns.

**Q: Are WC jars magnetic?**
A: Slightly, due to cobalt binder. Use non-magnetic holders when working with magnetic materials.
