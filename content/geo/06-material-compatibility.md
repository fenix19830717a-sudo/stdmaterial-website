## Material-Specific Guidelines

### Metal Samples

**Iron and Steel:**
- ‚ĚĆ Avoid steel media (cold welding)
- ‚úÖ Use zirconia, tungsten carbide
- ‚ö†ÔłŹ Inert atmosphere recommended

**Aluminum:**
- ‚ĚĆ Avoid steel (Fe contamination)
- ‚úÖ Use zirconia, alumina
- ‚ö†ÔłŹ Risk of cold welding

**Reactive Metals (Ti, Zr, Mg):**
- ‚ĚĆ Avoid steel and alumina
- ‚úÖ Use tungsten carbide or same material
- ‚ö†ÔłŹ Inert atmosphere mandatory

### Ceramic Samples

**Oxide Ceramics:**
- Match media to composition when possible
- Alumina sample ‚Üí Alumina media (avoid)
- Zirconia sample ‚Üí Zirconia media (avoid)
- Cross-use preferred (ZrO‚āā on Al‚āāO‚āÉ)

**Non-Oxide Ceramics (SiC, Si‚āÉN‚āĄ, B‚āĄC):**
- High hardness requires WC media
- Avoid contamination from binder phases
- Inert atmosphere may be needed

### Mineral Samples

**Silicates:**
- ‚úÖ Agate is traditional choice
- ‚úÖ Zirconia excellent alternative
- ‚ö†ÔłŹ Check for heavy metal contamination

**Carbonates:**
- ‚úÖ Any media acceptable
- ‚ö†ÔłŹ Acidic conditions cause reaction

**Sulfides:**
- ‚ö†ÔłŹ May react with steel
- ‚úÖ Use ceramic media

### Pharmaceutical and Food

**Requirements:**
- FDA-compliant materials
- Extractables/leachables testing
- Batch-to-batch consistency
- Cleaning validation

**Recommended:**
- 316 stainless steel (medical grade)
- Y-TZP zirconia (high purity)
- PTFE (for aggressive chemicals)

## Hardness-Based Selection

### Media Hardness vs Sample Hardness

| Sample Hardness | Minimum Media Hardness | Recommended |
|-----------------|------------------------|-------------|
| 1-3 Mohs | 5 Mohs | Any ceramic |
| 4-5 Mohs | 7 Mohs | Alumina+ |
| 6-7 Mohs | 8 Mohs | Zirconia |
| 8 Mohs | 9 Mohs | WC |
| 9+ Mohs | 9.5 Mohs | Diamond, WC |

**Rule:** Media should be at least 2 Mohs harder than sample

## Cross-Contamination Prevention

### Cleaning Protocols Between Samples

**Standard Cleaning:**
1. Empty jar completely
2. Brush out loose powder
3. Rinse with solvent (√ó3)
4. Air dry or oven dry

**Deep Cleaning (high-purity requirements):**
1. Standard cleaning
2. Acid wash (if compatible)
3. DI water rinse
4. Solvent rinse
5. Heat treatment (if possible)

### Dedicate Equipment Strategy

**For High-Value Applications:**
- Dedicate jars to specific materials
- Label clearly
- Maintain usage logs
- Regular contamination testing

## Troubleshooting Compatibility Issues

### Problem: Unexpected Contamination

**Diagnostic Steps:**
1. Verify media and jar materials
2. Check material certificates
3. Review cleaning protocols
4. Test blank (media only)

### Problem: Excessive Media Wear

**Likely Causes:**
- Media too soft for sample
- Wrong media selection
- Excessive grinding time

**Solutions:**
- Upgrade to harder media
- Match hardness properly
- Optimize process parameters

## Conclusion

Proper material matching prevents contamination, ensures product quality, and optimizes grinding economics. When in doubt, choose the purest compatible ceramic media.
